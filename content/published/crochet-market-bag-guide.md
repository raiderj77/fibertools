# How to Plan and Crochet a Market Bag

A crochet market bag is the project that makes strangers at the grocery store ask, "Did you make that?" These bags are practical, eco-friendly, and surprisingly strong. You can finish one in a weekend. The mesh stretches enough to hold a watermelon. Cotton lasts through years of washing. And the yarn costs under $10.

Here is how to plan a market bag from stitch choice to handle construction.

## What Makes a Good Market Bag?

A market bag needs four things. It needs stretch to fit different loads. It needs strength to hold 10 to 15 pounds without ripping. It needs to be washable because grocery bags get dirty. And it needs to store flat so it rolls up small when not in use.

**Mesh bags** stretch the most and pack down the smallest. The open network of chain spaces and single crochet nodes expands under weight. These bags are the lightest and fastest to crochet. The downside is that small items can fall through the holes.

**Solid-stitch bags** made with single crochet or half double crochet do not stretch as much. But they hold items of all sizes. They are heavier and use more yarn.

**Granny stitch bags** fall in the middle. Small clusters separated by chain spaces make a partly open fabric. It stretches a moderate amount and keeps most items inside.

## How Do You Size a Market Bag?

**Standard market bag dimensions:**
- Width: 14 to 16 inches, measured flat from edge to edge
- Height: 14 to 16 inches, from the base to the top of the bag, not including handles
- Base: 5 to 7 inches across, either flat and rectangular or round
- Handles: 9 to 12 inches long, from the bag top to the highest point of the handle

**Circumference at the top:** A 15-inch-wide bag has a 30-inch circumference. Your stitch count should produce this circumference at your gauge.

**The mesh stretch factor:** A mesh bag at rest might measure 12 inches wide. When loaded, it can stretch to 18 inches. Size mesh bags 10 to 15 percent smaller than your target loaded width.

## How Do You Crochet the Three Main Bag Styles?

### Mesh Market Bag

This is the classic stretchy grocery bag. You work it in the round from the base up.

**Base:** Chain 20 to 25. Single crochet around both sides of the chain to make an oval base. Work 3 to 4 rounds of single crochet. Increase at the ends to keep the base flat. The base should measure 5 to 6 inches by 2 to 3 inches.

**Body:** Switch to the mesh pattern. Work like this: chain 3, skip 2 stitches, single crochet in the next stitch. Repeat around. Work 20 to 25 rounds. The mesh creates a stretchy net.

**Handles:** At the top, chain 30 to 35 for each handle. Skip across the bag opening and join to the opposite side. Work 2 to 3 rows of single crochet over the chain to reinforce the handle. Make two handles, evenly spaced.

### Solid-Stitch Bag

This is a sturdy bag with no gaps. It works for all items. It is heavier and uses more yarn.

**Base:** Use the same oval base as the mesh bag, but make it a little larger at 6 to 7 inches.

**Body:** Continue in single crochet or half double crochet for 30 to 35 rounds. Do not increase. The straight tube forms the bag walls.

**Handles:** Use the same method as the mesh bag. But reinforce with 3 to 4 rows of single crochet. Solid bags hold more weight, so the handles need more reinforcement.

### Granny Stitch Bag

This bag uses clusters of 3 double crochets separated by chain 1 or chain 2 spaces. It creates a textured, partly open fabric.

**Base:** Make a solid single crochet oval base. Then switch to the granny stitch for the body.

**Body:** Work like this: 3 double crochets and chain 1 in the chain space of the previous round. Repeat. Work 18 to 22 rounds.

**Handles:** Make chain handles reinforced with 2 to 3 rows of single crochet and double crochet.

## What Yarn Works Best for Market Bags?

**Cotton is the standard choice.** It is strong, washable, and does not stretch permanently under a load. It also gets softer with each wash. Worsted weight cotton, listed as CYC 4, is the most popular option. Sugar'n Cream, Peaches and Creme, and Lion Brand 24/7 Cotton are all great choices. They cost about $3 to $5 per skein.

**Cotton and polyester blends** add durability and help the bag resist stretching. The polyester helps the bag spring back to shape after you load it.

**Avoid these yarns:**
- Acrylic stretches permanently under weight and cannot support heavy loads.
- Wool felts in the wash, so it is not practical for a grocery bag.
- Bamboo is too slippery for a structural bag.

**Yarn weight:** Worsted weight is the standard. DK weight makes a lighter bag that packs down more easily, but it is not as strong. Bulky weight creates a very heavy bag, which is more than you need for groceries.

## What Are Common Tips and Mistakes?

**Reinforce the handles.** Handles carry all the weight. Work at least 3 rows of single crochet over each handle chain. For extra strength, crochet the handle and then work a second pass of slip stitch crochet along the edges.

**Add a drawstring top (optional).** A chain-space row near the top lets you thread a drawstring cord through. Pull the drawstring to close the bag and keep items secure while you travel.

**Block the base.** The flat base determines how the bag sits when it is full. Block the base flat after you finish it. Soak it in water and pin it down. A well-blocked base helps the bag stand upright when loaded.

**Wash before first use.** Cotton yarn has sizing on it. Sizing is a starchy coating that makes the yarn stiff. Washing removes it and softens the fabric. The bag will feel and stretch better after that first wash.

**Common mistakes:**
- Not reinforcing handles, which causes them to break under heavy loads
- Using acrylic yarn, which stretches out and stays that way
- Making the base too small, which causes the bag to tip over when loaded
- Crocheting the mesh too tightly, which keeps the bag from stretching enough
- Making the handles too short, which makes them uncomfortable on your shoulder

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How strong is a crochet market bag?

A well-made cotton mesh bag holds 15 to 20 pounds with no trouble. Solid-stitch bags hold even more. The chain-loop mesh spreads weight evenly across the fabric. The handles are the weakest point, so always reinforce them with 3 or more rows of stitching. Cotton actually gets stronger when it is wet, which makes it a great choice for bags.

### How much yarn do I need for a market bag?

A mesh bag uses 150 to 200 yards of worsted weight cotton. A solid-stitch bag uses 300 to 400 yards. A granny stitch bag falls in between at 200 to 280 yards. Most worsted cotton skeins have 100 to 120 yards, so plan to buy 2 to 4 skeins depending on the style. Check the [Yarn Calculator](/yarn-calculator) for exact yardage at your gauge.

### Can I machine wash a crochet market bag?

Yes. Cotton crochet bags are machine washable. Use warm water and a gentle cycle. Tumble dry on low or lay the bag flat to dry. The bag may shrink 5 to 10 percent on the first wash. This actually tightens the mesh holes and makes the bag stronger. Wash it before you use it for the first time to remove the sizing.

### What's the best stitch for a market bag?

Chain-3 mesh is the most popular choice. You work it by repeating: chain 3, skip 2, single crochet. It stretches under a load, uses the least yarn, and packs down the smallest. If you need a bag that holds tiny items like loose beans, use chain-1 mesh or half double crochet for smaller openings. The best stitch depends on what you plan to carry.
