# Tunisian Crochet for Beginners

Tunisian crochet sits right between knitting and crochet. It uses a long hook like a knitting needle. It works stitches onto the hook like knitting. And it pulls through loops like crochet. The fabric it makes is thicker than regular crochet and denser than knitting. It has a woven texture that looks like nothing else.

If you crochet and have seen that weird long hook at the craft store, this guide is for you. Tunisian crochet is easier to learn than it looks. It also opens up a whole group of textures you cannot get any other way.

## What Is Tunisian Crochet?

Tunisian crochet (also called Afghan stitch) works in two passes per row.

**Forward pass:** Work across the row and pick up one loop per stitch. Put each loop on the hook. Do not pull through. When you reach the end, your hook holds 40, 60, or 100 or more loops, just like a knitting needle.

**Return pass:** Work back across the row by chaining through the loops. Yarn over and pull through 1 loop (the chain). Then yarn over and pull through 2 loops. Keep going until 1 loop remains. That finishes one row.

This two-pass system makes fabric that is double-thick compared to regular crochet. It does not curl as badly as stockinette knitting. It also has a grid-like structure that works great for colorwork, texture patterns, and blankets.

## What Hook Do You Need?

Tunisian crochet needs a special hook. It is longer than a regular crochet hook and has a stopper on the end. The stopper keeps loops from falling off. There are three types.

**Tunisian crochet hooks (straight):** These are 10 to 14 inches long with a stopper end. They work well for scarves and narrow projects up to about 14 inches wide.

**Tunisian hooks with cable extensions:** These have a short hook tip connected to a flexible cable, like circular knitting needles. They handle any width. They work best for blankets and wide projects. Buy these first.

**Double-ended Tunisian hooks:** These are pointed on both ends. They are used for working in the round. This is an advanced technique.

**Hook sizing:** Tunisian crochet makes denser fabric than regular crochet. Use a hook 1 to 2 sizes larger than you would normally use for the same yarn weight. If worsted weight calls for an I/9 (5.5mm) in regular crochet, try a J/10 (6.0mm) or K/10.5 (6.5mm) for Tunisian.

## How Do You Work the Three Basic Stitches?

### Tunisian Simple Stitch (TSS)

This is the foundation stitch. It makes fabric with small vertical bars on the front.

**Forward pass:** Insert the hook from right to left under the front vertical bar of the next stitch. Yarn over and pull up a loop. Leave it on the hook. Repeat across the row.

**Return pass:** Yarn over and pull through 1 loop. Then yarn over and pull through 2 loops. Repeat until 1 loop remains.

### Tunisian Knit Stitch (TKS)

This looks almost exactly like stockinette knitting. It is the most popular Tunisian stitch for blankets and garments.

**Forward pass:** Insert the hook from front to back between the front and back vertical bars. Go through the stitch, not under the bar. Yarn over and pull up a loop. Repeat across.

**Return pass:** Same as TSS.

### Tunisian Purl Stitch (TPS)

This makes a bumpy, textured surface like garter stitch or purl knitting.

**Forward pass:** Bring the yarn to the front. Insert the hook under the front vertical bar from right to left. Yarn over from behind and pull up a loop. Repeat across.

**Return pass:** Same as TSS.

You can mix TKS and TPS in the same row. This lets you create ribbing, seed stitch, and other texture patterns, just like knitting.

## What Projects Work Best for Tunisian Crochet?

**Blankets and afghans.** The dense, woven-look fabric is great for warm blankets. Tunisian blankets hold their shape without blocking and lay flat on their own. A worsted weight Tunisian blanket at 50x60 inches uses about 2,800 to 3,200 yards. That is slightly more than regular crochet.

**Scarves and cowls.** The thick fabric makes a warm, structured scarf that does not stretch out. Use Tunisian knit stitch for a knit-look scarf without needing to know how to knit.

**Washcloths and dishcloths.** Cotton yarn in Tunisian simple stitch makes a dense, absorbent cloth with great scrubbing texture. A 9x9 inch cloth takes about 80 to 100 yards of worsted cotton.

**Entrelac.** Tunisian crochet is the traditional technique for entrelac. Entrelac is a woven-look pattern made of squares that tilt at 45 degrees. Each square is worked separately in Tunisian stitch. You pick up stitches from the edge of the square before it.

## What Are Common Tips and Mistakes?

**Go up 1 to 2 hook sizes.** Tunisian fabric is naturally tight. If your fabric is stiff and will not drape, your hook is too small. Size up until the fabric feels soft enough to wrap around your shoulders.

**Keep loose loops on the forward pass.** If you pull each loop tight as you pick it up, the fabric scrunches and curls. Keep loops the same height as your chain, roughly 1/4 to 1/2 inch tall on the hook.

**Count after every row.** The most common mistake is skipping the last stitch on the forward pass. The last vertical bar sits right next to the edge and is easy to miss. Count your loops before starting the return pass. The number should match your stitch count.

**Block Tunisian crochet flat.** Tunisian fabric curls to the right and toward the back. Wet blocking flattens it. Pin it to a mat, mist with water or soak it, and let it dry flat. After blocking, the curl goes away and the stitch pattern shows clearly.

**Common mistakes:**
- Using a regular crochet hook (too short, loops fall off)
- Not going up in hook size (fabric turns out stiff as cardboard)
- Pulling the return pass too tight (causes the fabric to get narrower)
- Skipping the last stitch on the forward pass (the piece loses 1 stitch per row)
- Not blocking (the curl hides the beautiful stitch texture)

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I need a special hook for Tunisian crochet?

Yes. A regular crochet hook is too short to hold all the loops from the forward pass. You need a Tunisian hook (10 to 14 inches long with a stopper) or a hook with a cable extension. Cable-extension hooks are the most useful because they handle any project width. They cost $8 to $15 each or $25 to $40 for a set.

### Is Tunisian crochet harder than regular crochet?

No, it is just different. If you can chain, insert your hook, and pull up loops, you can do Tunisian. The forward pass is picking up loops. The return pass is pulling through 2 loops over and over. The rhythm takes about 10 to 15 minutes to learn. The unusual part is holding many loops on the hook at once, which feels like knitting.

### Does Tunisian crochet use more yarn than regular crochet?

Yes, about 10 to 20% more. The double-thick fabric uses extra yarn per square inch. A regular crochet blanket using 2,400 yards of worsted weight might need 2,700 to 2,900 yards in Tunisian. Plan for this when you buy yarn and add 15% extra to be safe.

### Why does my Tunisian crochet curl?

Tunisian fabric naturally curls to the right and toward the back. This happens because the forward and return passes create different tensions on each side. Blocking fixes it completely. Wet block by soaking the piece, pinning it flat, and letting it dry. For stubborn curl, try a slightly larger hook or alternate Tunisian knit and purl rows to balance the tension.
