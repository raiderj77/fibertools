# How to Crochet a Granny Square Step by Step

To crochet a granny square, start by making a center square with double crochet stitches. Then work outward in rounds. You will alternate between corner stitches and diagonal stitches.

## How Does a Granny Square Work?

A granny square is a classic crochet pattern. You work in rounds to build a square shape. Each round adds new stitches on top of the round before it.

To make a granny square, you need to know a few basic stitches. These are the chain stitch, slip stitch, and double crochet. You also need to know how to increase and decrease stitches. This shapes the square and gives you a clean join. You start at the center and work outward, round by round.

## What Are the Basic Stitches Used in a Granny Square?

The basic stitches used in a granny square include:

* Chain stitch (ch): a foundation stitch that makes a row of chain links
* Slip stitch (sl st): a stitch used to join rounds together
* Double crochet (dc): a stitch used to make the body of the square
* Half double crochet (hdc): an optional stitch that makes a slightly denser fabric

You can combine these stitches in many ways. This lets you make many different granny square patterns. Once you know the basic stitches, you can create a wide variety of designs.

## How Do You Join a Granny Square to Another Square?

To join two granny squares together, you need to learn a seamless join. One common way to do this is the slip stitch method. You insert your crochet hook through the top of one square and through the bottom of the other square.

Follow these steps to join two squares using the slip stitch method:

1. Find the top and bottom of each square.
2. Insert the crochet hook through the top of one square and through the bottom of the other square.
3. Pull the yarn through to make a slip stitch.
4. Fasten off by working a slip stitch around the edges of the square. This creates a clean, seamless join.
