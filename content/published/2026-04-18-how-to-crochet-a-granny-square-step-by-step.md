# How to Crochet a Granny Square Step by Step

To crochet a granny square, start by creating a center square with double crochet stitches, and then work your way outward in rounds, alternating between corner and diagonal stitches.

## How Does a Granny Square Work?

A granny square is a classic crochet pattern that involves working in rounds to create a square shape. It consists of a series of rounds, each one building on the previous round by adding new stitches to the center square.

To create a granny square, you will need to know how to work basic crochet stitches such as chain, slip stitch, and double crochet. You will also need to understand how to increase and decrease stitches to shape the square and create a seamless join. The granny square pattern is typically worked in rounds, starting from the center of the square and working outwards.

## What Are the Basic Stitches Used in a Granny Square?

The basic stitches used in a granny square include:

* Chain stitch (ch): a foundation stitch that creates a series of chain links
* Slip stitch (sl st): a stitch used to join rounds together
* Double crochet (dc): a stitch used to create the body of the square
* Half double crochet (hdc): an optional stitch used to create a slightly denser stitch

These stitches can be combined in various ways to create a range of different granny square patterns. By understanding the basic stitches and how to work them together, you can create a wide variety of granny square designs.

## How Do You Join a Granny Square to Another Square?

To join a granny square to another square, you will need to learn how to work a seamless join. This can be done by using a special technique called the slip stitch method, which involves inserting the crochet hook through the top of one square and through the bottom of another square.

To join a granny square to another square using the slip stitch method, follow these steps:

1. Identify the top and bottom of each square
2. Insert the crochet hook through the top of one square and through the bottom of another square
3. Pull the yarn through to create a slip stitch
4. Fasten off the square by working a slip stitch around the edges of the square to create a seamless join
