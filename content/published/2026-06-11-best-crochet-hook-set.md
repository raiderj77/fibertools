# Best Crochet Hook Set: What Actually Lives in My Hook Roll

**The short answer:** For most crocheters, the [Clover Amour set](https://www.clovermfg.com/) covers sizes B-1 (2.25 mm) through K-10.5 (6.5 mm) with ergonomic handles that hold up through long project sessions. If budget is the priority, the Boye Aluminum set gives you a similar size range for under $15. Neither is perfect for every project, and your hands will have opinions too.

## What sizes should a crochet hook set actually include?

A good set needs to cover at least 2.25 mm through 6.5 mm. That range works for most worsted, DK, and sport weight patterns. Beyond that, you want a 9 mm and a 12 mm for bulky yarn. You also want something in the 1.5 to 1.75 mm range if you do thread crochet or Tunisian with fine yarn.

The standard US size letter system goes from B-1 (2.25 mm) up through S (19 mm). But most sets you can buy stop around N or P. That is fine for everyday use. The problem people run into is the jump between 6.5 mm and the next size up in a cheap kit. Budget sets sometimes skip 5.5 mm and 6.0 mm entirely. That matters when a pattern calls for those exact sizes. Check the mm markings, not just the letter labels. There is [some inconsistency between brands on the letter-to-mm conversion](https://www.craftyarncouncil.com/standards/hook-and-needle-sizes).

If you work with thread, like size 10 cotton, you need steel hooks. Steel hooks are sold separately from aluminum or plastic sets. Steel hooks run from size 00 (3.5 mm) down to size 14 (0.75 mm). The sizing runs backward compared to standard hooks. A set like the [Tulip Etimo steel hooks](https://www.tulipco.net/) covers that range with a soft-grip handle style.

## Ergonomic or inline, does the handle shape really matter?

Yes, and it matters more than most beginners expect. Tapered, or inline, hooks have the hook head in line with the shaft. Ergonomic hooks have a wider handle and a slightly different head shape. Neither is better. But they feel very different after two hours of work. They can also produce a different stitch appearance for some crocheters.

The [Furls Streamline both have handles wide enough](/blog/best-crochet-hooks/) to reduce the pinching grip that causes wrist ache. The Clover Amour does too. The [Arthritis Foundation has noted](https://www.arthritis.org/living-with-arthritis/tools-resources/arthritis-today/features/crochet-with-arthritis.php) that wider, cushioned handles reduce joint strain for people with hand issues. That applies to healthy hands doing a lot of work too.

Inline hooks, like the Susan Bates Silvalume, are what most older patterns were written with. Some crocheters find their stitches look tighter and more even with them. If you learned on inline hooks, switching to ergonomic in the middle of a project can change your tension. Measure as you go. Check your gauge before you pick a hook style for a fitted garment.

But the jump from $40 to $120 does not make most people's finished work much better.

## Do I need a separate set for Tunisian crochet?

Tunisian crochet needs a longer hook. Usually 9 to 14 inches long. It also needs a stopper or cable on the end to hold the live loops. Standard hooks max out around 6 inches. They will not work for wide Tunisian panels. You need either a fixed long Tunisian hook or an interchangeable Tunisian hook with extension cables.

The [Clover Tunisian set](https://www.clovermfg.com/) includes hooks with removable cables. It covers sizes E through K. For larger sizes, ChiaoGoo makes Tunisian hooks in 5.5 mm through 10 mm with flexible cables. If you are just trying Tunisian for the first time, buy one 5 mm or 5.5 mm Tunisian hook before spending money on a full set.

## What should I look for in a hook case or storage?

The case matters more than people think. Hooks stored loose in a bag can get bent tips and scratched shafts. A scratched shaft will snag yarn. A roll case with individual slots keeps hooks separated. The Clover Amour set comes with a zippered case that works well. If you buy hooks one at a time, the [Намaste Maker's Bag](https://www.namastemade.com/) and similar roll-style organizers hold mixed sizes without hooks rattling together.

Label your case with the mm size, not just the letter. When you are in the middle of a project at 11 pm looking for your 5.5 mm hook, you will be glad you did.

## Frequently asked questions

### What is the [best crochet hook set for beginners](/blog/best-crochet-hooks-for-beginners/)?
The best crochet hook set for beginners includes a range of sizes, usually 2 mm to 10 mm. It should have ergonomic handles and a storage case. Sets with soft-grip rubber handles reduce hand fatigue during long projects. That makes them great for new crocheters still building their skills. Look for sets that also include a few basic extras like stitch markers and a tapestry needle. That way you do not have to buy those things separately.

### What size crochet hooks should a complete set include?
A complete set should include sizes from 2 mm to 12 mm. That covers the widest range of yarn weights. Smaller hooks, 2 mm to 3.5 mm, work with fine and lace-weight yarns. Mid-range hooks, 4 mm to 6 mm, work with popular worsted and DK weights. Larger hooks, 8 mm to 12 mm, handle bulky and super-bulky yarns. Having this full range means you are ready for any pattern without buying extra hooks.

### Are ergonomic crochet hooks worth it?
Yes, ergonomic crochet hooks are worth it. This is especially true if you crochet for long stretches of time. Standard metal or plastic hooks can cause hand cramps and strain over time. Ergonomic hooks have wider, cushioned handles. These spread out the grip pressure more evenly and reduce fatigue. People with arthritis or carpal tunnel syndrome benefit from them a lot. Many hook sets now include ergonomic handles as a standard feature. You can find them at many price points.

### What is the difference between inline and tapered crochet hooks?
Inline hooks have a hook head that sits in line with the shaft. Tapered hooks have a more rounded head that sits above the shaft. Inline hooks, like the Bates style, tend to grab yarn more precisely. They work well for crocheters with tighter tension. Tapered hooks, like the Boye style, glide through stitches more smoothly. They work well for looser tension. Personal preference plays a big role. Trying both styles helps you find the one that fits you best.

### How do I store and organize a crochet hook set?
The best way to store a crochet hook set is in a case or roll-up pouch. It should keep hooks separated, labeled, and protected. Many sets come with a zippered case or snap-closure holder with individual slots for each size. For bigger collections, a standing desktop organizer or a labeled fabric roll works well. Keeping hooks organized by size protects the tips and saves time when you need to switch sizes mid-project.
