# Brioche Knitting for Beginners

Brioche knitting makes fabric so thick and squishy that even non-knitters will pick it up and squeeze it. The ribbing is twice as deep as regular ribbing. In two-color brioche, each side of the fabric shows the opposite color combination. That gives you two scarves in one.

The technique looks hard. The abbreviations (brk, sl1yo, brp) look like a foreign language. But brioche is built on just two moves. First, slip a stitch with a yarn over. Then knit or purl two together on the next row. Once those two moves click, you can brioche anything.

## What Is Brioche Knitting?

Brioche is a knitting technique. You work every other stitch on each row. You slip the stitches in between, each with a yarn over. On the next row, you knit each slipped stitch together with its yarn over. This creates a plump, pillowy stitch that is twice as thick as stockinette.

**Fisherman's rib** is the one-color version. It looks just like brioche. But it uses a different method. You knit into the row below instead of slipping with yarn overs. The result is the same: thick, squishy, reversible 1x1 ribbing.

**Two-color brioche** uses two yarns. You alternate colors each row. One color dominates the front. The other dominates the back. Flip the fabric over and the colors switch. This is what makes brioche scarves and cowls so impressive. You get two distinct looks in one project.

## How Do You Work Basic One-Color Brioche?

### Setup Row
Cast on an odd number of stitches. Row 1 (setup): Knit 1, slip 1 with yarn in front and yarn over (sl1yo). Repeat across. End with knit 1.

### The Two Repeating Rows

**Row 2:** Slip 1 with yarn over (sl1yo), then brk (knit the slipped stitch together with its yarn over). Repeat across.

**Row 3:** Brk, then slip 1 with yarn over (sl1yo). Repeat across.

Alternate Rows 2 and 3. That is it. Two moves: sl1yo and brk. The rhythm becomes automatic after 6 to 8 rows.

### Two-Color Brioche

Work each row twice. Use Color A first, sliding the stitches back to the start of the needle. Then use Color B. You need double-pointed needles or a circular needle so you can work from both ends.

**Row 1A (Color A):** sl1yo, brk. Repeat.
**Row 1B (Color B):** brp (brioche purl), sl1yo. Repeat.

The two colors create contrasting ribs on each side. The front shows Color A ribs on a Color B background. The back shows Color B ribs on a Color A background.

## What Yarn Works Best for Brioche?

**Smooth, plied yarns** show brioche stitch definition most clearly. Look for tightly plied merino, merino blends, or superwash wool in DK to worsted weight.

**Avoid:** Fuzzy yarns like mohair or brushed alpaca hide the stitch definition. Singles yarns (one-ply) twist unevenly in brioche and make uneven ribs. Cotton has no stretch and makes flat, lifeless brioche. Variegated yarns blur the clean rib lines.

**For two-color brioche:** Pick two colors with high contrast. One should be light and one should be dark. The contrast makes the color-switching ribs stand out. Low contrast makes the two-color effect hard to see.

**Yarn weight sweet spot:** DK (CYC 3) or worsted (CYC 4). Fingering weight brioche takes a long time because the fabric grows slowly. Bulky weight brioche looks great but ends up very thick and heavy.

## What Are Common Tips and Mistakes?

**Swatch in brioche, not stockinette.** Your brioche gauge will be completely different. A stockinette swatch is useless for planning a brioche project.

**Count stitches by pairs.** In brioche, each stitch is really a pair. It includes the knit column plus the purl column behind it. When a pattern says 40 stitches, you have 40 visible knit columns. But you have 80 loops on your needle, including the yarn overs. Count the knit columns, not the loops.

**Use lifelines.** Brioche is hard to frog cleanly. The yarn overs tangle with the slipped stitches. It is easy to lose a stitch when ripping back. Thread a piece of scrap yarn through all stitches every 10 to 15 rows. If you make a mistake, you can rip back to the lifeline safely.

**Do not pull the yarn over tight.** A tight yarn over scrunches the brioche and makes it stiff. Let the yarn over sit loosely on the needle. The brk stitch on the next row absorbs the slack and creates the plump look.

**Common mistakes:**
- Forgetting the yarn over on a sl1yo (drops a stitch on the next row)
- Working sl1yo at the beginning of a row when it should be brk, or the other way around
- Using stockinette gauge for calculations (brioche is about 50% narrower)
- Not alternating which end of the needle you start from in two-color brioche
- Choosing two colors too close in value (the two-color effect disappears)

## What Do Real Brioche Projects Look Like?

**The one-color cowl.** A knitter made a brioche cowl in worsted weight merino. She cast on 100 stitches around on a 24-inch circular needle. The thick, squishy fabric measured 9 inches tall and 22 inches around. She used 310 yards, about 80% more than a regular ribbed cowl the same size. The project took 12 hours. The reversible fabric looked equally good on both sides.

**The two-color scarf.** A knitter used DK weight yarn in charcoal and gold. She made a two-color brioche scarf that was 35 stitches wide (70 loops) and 62 inches long. The front showed charcoal ribs on a gold background. The back showed gold ribs on a charcoal background. She used 420 yards of each color, 840 yards total. The project took 25 hours. The scarf became her most complimented accessory.

**The brioche hat.** A knitter worked a one-color brioche hat from the brim up in bulky weight yarn. She cast on 56 stitches on double-pointed needles, worked 7 inches, then decreased. Total yarn used: 140 yards. The thick brioche fabric made the hat very warm without needing a lining.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does brioche really use twice as much yarn as regular knitting?

Roughly yes. Each brioche stitch includes a yarn over. That doubles the yarn used per stitch compared to plain knitting. A stockinette scarf using 250 yards needs about 450 to 500 yards in brioche at the same size. Always multiply your regular yarn estimate by 1.8 to 2.0 for brioche projects.

### Is brioche knitting hard to learn?

The technique has only two moves: sl1yo and brk. Learning those takes about 30 minutes. The harder parts are reading your fabric, catching mistakes early, and managing two colors for two-color brioche. Start with a one-color swatch of 21 stitches. Once the rhythm clicks after 8 to 10 rows, everything else builds on those same two moves.

### Can I do brioche in crochet?

Not exactly. There is no direct crochet version of brioche knitting. But "brioche crochet," also called slip stitch ribbing, creates a similar thick ribbed fabric. It uses front post and back post slip stitches to mimic the knit-purl rib look. It is thicker and stiffer than knit brioche.

### Why does my brioche fabric look wrong on one side?

In two-color brioche, if one side looks messy and the other looks clean, you are probably working sl1yo and brk on the wrong rows for one of the colors. Check that Color A always does the same operation, either brk or brp. Color B should always do the opposite. Staying consistent between colors creates clean rib separation.
