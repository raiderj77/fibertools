# Cross Stitch Size Chart: How Big Will Your Finished Piece Actually Be?

**The short answer:** Finished cross stitch size depends on two numbers. The first is your fabric count, which is the number of stitches per inch. The second is how many stitches your design uses. Divide your design stitches by the fabric count to get inches. A 100x140 stitch design on 14-count Aida comes out to about 7.1" x 10". That is the whole formula. Everything below helps you use it with confidence.

---

## What does "fabric count" actually mean?

Fabric count is [the number of stitches (or threads) per inch](/blog/cross-stitch-fabric-count/). A higher count means smaller stitches and a finer finished piece. A lower count means bigger, bolder work.

The most common counts are 11, 14, 16, 18, and 28. Aida cloth comes in all of these. [DMC's fabric guide](https://www.dmc.com/us/fabric) says 14-count is the best choice for beginners. The holes are easy to see and the needle moves through cleanly. If you have been stitching for a while and want a more delicate look, try 18-count or 28-count evenweave stitched over two threads. You get a finer result without changing your thread count or design.

---

## How do I calculate finished size from stitch count?

Divide your stitch count by the fabric count to get finished inches. A 50x70 design on 14-count Aida works out to 3.6 inches wide by 5 inches tall. The same design on 11-count grows to 4.5 by 6.4 inches. Fabric count is the single biggest way to control size.

Divide the stitch width by the fabric count to get width in inches. Do the same for height.

**Formula:**
- Width (inches) = Stitch width / Fabric count
- Height (inches) = Stitch height / Fabric count

**Quick examples:**

| Design (stitches) | Fabric Count | Finished Size |
|---|---|---|
| 50 x 70 | 11-count | 4.5" x 6.4" |
| 50 x 70 | 14-count | 3.6" x 5" |
| 50 x 70 | 18-count | 2.8" x 3.9" |
| 100 x 140 | 14-count | 7.1" x 10" |
| 100 x 140 | 18-count | 5.6" x 7.8" |
| 200 x 200 | 14-count | 14.3" x 14.3" |
| 200 x 200 | 28-count (over 2) | 14.3" x 14.3" |

Notice that 28-count evenweave stitched over two threads gives the same finished size as 14-count Aida. That is not a coincidence. It is how the math works out. This is why many experienced stitchers switch to linen and evenweave without giving up the sizing they are used to.

## What fabric count should I choose for my project?

Match the count to how you will use the piece and how much detail your design needs. A sampler going into a 5x7 frame needs different math than a large portrait meant for a 16x20 frame.

Here is a simple way to think about it:

- **11-count:** Great for kids' projects, ornaments meant to be seen from a distance, or anyone new to the craft. Stitches are large and forgiving.
- **14-count:** The most popular choice. Most store-bought patterns are made with 14-count in mind. Use this count unless you have a reason to pick something else.
- **16-count:** Falls between 14 and 18. Useful when you want a slightly smaller piece without jumping all the way to 18-count.
- **18-count:** Good for fine detail, miniatures, and jewelry inserts. You will want good lighting and maybe a magnifier.
- **28-count evenweave (over 2):** Gives the same finished size as 14-count but with a softer, more fabric-like feel. Common for heirloom pieces.
- **32-count and higher:** Advanced work. Tiny stitches, great detail, and slow going.

## How much extra fabric do I need around my design?

Add at least 3 inches on every side of your design before you cut. That extra fabric grips your hoop while you stitch. It also wraps around a frame when you mount the finished piece. Cutting too close saves nothing and usually ruins the project at the end.

Add at least 3 inches on each side of your design. That gives you 6 extra inches in both width and height.

This is not just for looks. You need fabric to fit in a hoop or frame while you stitch. You also need it when you mount or frame the finished piece. [Dimensions Crafts recommends](https://www.dimensionscrafts.com/) at least 3 inches of border on all sides for framing. If you plan to frame under glass with a mat, 4 inches gives you more room to work.

So if your finished stitched area is 7" x 10", cut your fabric at least 13" x 16". Mark the center of your fabric before you start. Fold it in quarters and mark with a washable pen or a single long basting stitch. Most patterns mark their center too. Matching those two points keeps your design from drifting to one side.

## Does thread count or number of strands change the finished size?

No, strand count has zero effect on finished size. Size is set only by your fabric count and stitch count. Strands only change how full and textured the stitching looks. Two strands on 14-count Aida is the standard starting point for solid, clean coverage on most everyday projects.

No, [strands don't change finished size](/blog/how-many-cross-stitches-per-skein/). They change coverage and texture.

The size of your piece comes from fabric count and stitch count only. Strands affect how solid and raised the stitching looks. [DMC's standard recommendation](https://www.dmc.com/us/needlework-tips) is 2 strands on 14-count Aida for a clean, full look. On 11-count you might use 3 strands. On 18-count, 1 strand is often enough. Using more strands than your count calls for makes stitches look crowded. It can also warp the fabric over time.

## Can I resize a pattern by changing fabric count?

Yes, and this is one of the most useful things to know. Changing fabric count is the easiest way to make a design bigger or smaller without editing the pattern at all.

Say you have a 120 x 120 stitch pattern. On 14-count that is 8.6" square. Want it smaller for a card insert? Stitch it on 18-count and it drops to 6.7" square. Want it bigger for a pillow front? Use 11-count and it becomes 10.9" square.

The design does not change at all. You are just changing the physical size of each stitch by picking a different fabric. Run the numbers before you buy fabric. That way you will not be surprised when the finished piece comes off the hoop.

## Frequently asked questions

### How do I calculate the finished size of a cross stitch project?
Divide the total stitch count by the fabric count to get the size in inches. For example, a design that is 140 stitches wide on 14-count Aida will measure 10 inches across (140 divided by 14 equals 10). Always add a border of at least 2 to 3 inches of extra fabric on each side for framing or finishing.

### What is the most common Aida fabric count for beginners?
14-count Aida is the most popular choice for beginners. The holes are easy to see and the stitches are a comfortable size to work with. It makes a finished stitch size of about 1/14 of an inch. Most beginner cross stitch kits are made for 14-count fabric.

### How does fabric count affect the finished size of a cross stitch design?
Higher fabric counts make smaller, more detailed finished pieces. Lower counts make larger, chunkier stitches. The same 100-stitch design on 18-count Aida will measure about 5.5 inches. On 28-count evenweave stitched over two threads it will measure about 4 inches. Picking your fabric count is one of the easiest ways to resize any pattern.

### How much fabric do I need for a cross stitch project?
Take your design's stitch dimensions and divide by your fabric count to get inches. Then add at least 4 to 6 inches total, which is 2 to 3 inches per side, for a border. This extra fabric is needed for hooping, framing, or finishing without cutting into your stitched design. It is always better to start with a little more fabric than you think you need.

### Can I use a cross stitch size chart for different fabric types like linen or evenweave?
Yes, the same size formula works for linen and evenweave fabrics. But these are usually stitched over two threads instead of one. That means 28-count linen stitched over two threads gives the same stitch size as 14-count Aida. Always check whether a pattern calls for stitching over one or two threads before you calculate your finished size using a size chart.
