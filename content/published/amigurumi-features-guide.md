# How to Add Eyes and Features to Crochet Amigurumi

The shape of an amigurumi gives it a body. The features give it personality. Two safety eyes placed 1 stitch apart create a different character than eyes placed 6 stitches apart. A tiny embroidered mouth turns a blank face into something that makes people smile. The features are what turn a crocheted blob into a bear, cat, bunny, or totally original creature.

Getting eyes and features right can feel scary for most amigurumi makers. Here is how to choose, place, and attach features that bring your creations to life.

> **A mistake I made:** My circles curled into bowls and my bowls flattened into plates. A flat circle needs the right number of increases each round. The [circle calculator](/circle-calculator) gives you the counts, and the [amigurumi shapes guide](/amigurumi-shapes) helps with toys.

## What Are the Options for Amigurumi Eyes?

### Safety Eyes (Most Popular)

Safety eyes are plastic eyes with a post. The post pushes through the crochet fabric and locks in place with a washer on the inside. They are the most common choice for amigurumi and stuffed toys.

**Pros:** Clean, professional look. Secure when put in the right way. Available in 4mm to 30mm sizes in black, colored, and glitter finishes. Easy to place and adjust before locking.

**Cons:** Not safe for children under 3 years old. They are a choking hazard if the washer fails. You cannot remove or move them after the washer is locked. You must install them before stuffing.

**Common sizes by amigurumi height:**
- Mini amigurumi (2 to 4 inches): 6mm eyes
- Small (4 to 6 inches): 8 to 10mm eyes
- Medium (6 to 10 inches): 10 to 15mm eyes
- Large (10 or more inches): 15 to 24mm eyes

### Embroidered Eyes

These eyes are stitched right onto the fabric with yarn or embroidery floss. They are completely safe for babies and toddlers. You can make them in almost any design.

**Techniques:**
- **Satin stitch:** Parallel straight stitches that fill an oval or circle shape. Creates smooth, flat eyes.
- **French knots:** Wrap yarn around the needle 2 to 3 times and pull through. Creates small, round dot eyes.
- **Chain stitch:** Outline circles or create spirals for expressive cartoon eyes.

**Best for:** Baby toys, artistic or stylized amigurumi, and designs where you want a specific look that plastic eyes cannot give you.

### Felt or Fabric Eyes

Cut these from felt and glue or sew them onto the amigurumi. This allows for complex shapes like cat eyes, anime eyes, or sleepy eyes. Those shapes are hard to get with plastic eyes or embroidery.

**Attach with:** Fabric glue, which is fast but less durable, or hand-stitching, which is slower but permanent.

### Button Eyes

A classic choice for vintage-style toys. Not safe for children under 3. Sew them on with strong thread through the amigurumi body. Pull slightly to create an indented eye socket.

## How Do You Place Eyes Correctly?

Eye placement changes the whole personality of a character. The same body with different eye spacing can look happy, sad, surprised, or suspicious.

**General placement rules:**

**Vertical position:** Place eyes between rounds 8 and 12 on a standard sphere head that has 18 to 20 rounds total. Eyes placed higher on the head look younger and cuter. Eyes placed lower look more mature or animal-like.

**Horizontal spacing:** For a cute look, place eyes 4 to 6 stitches apart. For a wide-eyed look, place them 7 to 10 stitches apart. For a close-set, suspicious look, place them 2 to 3 stitches apart.

**The pin test:** Before installing safety eyes or embroidering, stick pins through the fabric at the planned eye spots. Stuff the head lightly and look at the face from the front. Move the pins until you like the expression. Only then install the permanent eyes.

## How Do You Install Safety Eyes?

1. **Mark the position** with pins or stitch markers on the crocheted head. Do this before the head is finished. Leave the last 4 to 6 rounds unworked.
2. **Push the eye post** through the crochet fabric from the outside to the inside.
3. **Slide the washer** onto the post from the inside. Push it down firmly until it clicks and locks. You will hear or feel a snap.
4. **Check security** by pulling the eye from the front. It should not move, wiggle, or pull through.
5. **Finish and stuff** the head after the eyes are installed.

**The order matters:** Install safety eyes BEFORE closing and stuffing the head. You cannot push the eye post through fabric that is already stuffed. You also cannot attach the washer from inside a closed sphere.

## What Are the Best Tips and Common Mistakes?

**Symmetry is everything.** Count stitches from a center point to each eye. Use something like the start-of-round marker as your center. If the left eye is 4 stitches from center, the right eye must also be 4 stitches from center on the other side. Eyes that are not even make the face look off, even if you cannot explain why.

**Embroider on a stuffed piece.** Stuff the head before embroidering features. This is the opposite of what you do with safety eyes. Embroidering on flat, unstuffed fabric causes problems. When you add stuffing, the face stretches and the features shift.

**Use a long needle for pulling features.** For indented features like eye sockets, dimples, or smile lines, use a long doll-making needle. Enter at the eye position and exit at the back of the head. Pull firmly to indent the fabric. Then return and secure the thread. This sculpting method adds 3D depth to flat faces.

**Add a highlight to embroidered eyes.** Make one tiny white straight stitch in the upper corner of a black embroidered eye. This small detail makes the difference between eyes that look flat and eyes that look alive. The highlight looks like a light reflection and makes the character seem animated.

**Common mistakes:**
- Installing safety eyes after stuffing. The washer cannot be attached this way.
- Placing eyes too close to the edge of the head. They shift toward the side when stuffed.
- Using eyes too large for the head. They overwhelm the face.
- Not testing placement with pins first. Permanent eyes cannot be moved.
- Forgetting to count stitches for symmetry. One eye ends up higher or further to the side than the other.
- Using safety eyes for baby toys. They are not safe for children under 3.

## What Do Real Amigurumi Feature Projects Look Like?

**The classic teddy bear.** One crocheter made a 10-inch bear in worsted weight yarn. She placed 12mm safety eyes at round 10 of an 18-round sphere head, 6 stitches apart. She embroidered a triangular nose with satin stitch in black yarn using 5 rows of parallel stitches. She added a small curved mouth in backstitch beneath the nose. The pin test took 3 adjustments before she committed to the eye placement.

**The baby-safe bunny.** One crocheter made a gift for a 1-year-old and could not use safety eyes. She embroidered closed sleepy eyes with two curved lines in backstitch using black embroidery floss. She added French knot nostrils and a small V-shaped mouth. The result was cute and completely safe.

**The anime-style cat.** One crocheter wanted large, expressive anime eyes that safety eyes could not replicate. She cut oval shapes from white felt and drew the iris and pupil with fabric markers. Then she hand-stitched them to the head. She added small white felt triangles above each eye for highlights. The oversized felt eyes gave the cat a dramatic, stylized look.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are safety eyes safe for babies?

Safety eyes are not recommended for children under 3 years old. The plastic post and washer can potentially be pulled free with enough force. For baby toys, use embroidered eyes, crocheted eyes, or appliqued felt. Crocheted eyes are small circles stitched flat onto the face. Any feature that cannot be pulled off the surface is baby-safe.

### When do I install safety eyes during construction?

Install safety eyes after crocheting past the eye placement row. Do this before closing and stuffing the head. Leave the last 4 to 6 rounds of the head unworked. Push the eye posts through and attach the washers from inside. Check the placement. Then finish the remaining rounds and stuff. You need to reach inside the head to attach the washers.

### How do I fix asymmetric eye placement?

If you used safety eyes, you cannot move them once the washer is locked. Use pliers to gently pry the washer off. It may break. Remove the eye and install a new one in the correct spot. For embroidered eyes, carefully cut the stitches, remove the thread, and re-embroider. The best fix is prevention. Always test with pins first.

### What size safety eyes should I use?

Match the eye size to the amigurumi head diameter. As a general rule, eyes should be about 10 to 15% of the head's widest diameter. A head that is 3 inches wide, about 30 stitches around, looks best with 9 to 12mm eyes. A head that is 2 inches wide suits 6 to 8mm eyes. Eyes that are too large overwhelm the face. Eyes that are too small get lost. Test a few sizes with pins before you commit.

## Bring Your Amigurumi to Life

The features are where personality happens. Take your time with placement. Test with pins before committing. Do not be afraid to try embroidered details beyond just eyes. A simple curved line for a mouth can make the difference between a blob and a character.
