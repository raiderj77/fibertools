# How to Knit or Crochet a Blanket Scarf

A blanket scarf is exactly what it sounds like. It is a scarf big enough to work as a blanket too. Wrap it once around your neck and it hangs down to your waist. Fold it in half and wrap it, and you get a thick, warm cowl. Lay it flat and it covers your lap on a cold airplane. You can also drape it over your shoulders like a shawl.

The big size makes it one of the most useful accessories you can make. The simple shape, a rectangle, makes it one of the easiest. The main thing to plan is the size and how much yarn you need.

## What Size Should a Blanket Scarf Be?

Blanket scarves are wider and longer than regular scarves. The extra width is what makes them so useful.

| Style | Width | Length |
|---|---|---|
| Regular scarf | 6 to 8 inches | 60 to 70 inches |
| Wide scarf | 10 to 12 inches | 70 to 80 inches |
| **Blanket scarf** | **16 to 24 inches** | **70 to 90 inches** |
| Oversized wrap | 24 to 30 inches | 80 to 100 inches |

**The best size for most blanket scarves:** 20 inches wide by 80 inches long. This size wraps well around the neck. It has enough drape to use as a shawl. It also does not look too big on smaller frames.

**For petite frames:** 16 to 18 inches wide, 70 to 75 inches long.
**For larger frames or more coverage:** 24 inches wide, 85 to 90 inches long.

## How Much Yarn Does a Blanket Scarf Need?

Blanket scarves use 2 to 4 times more yarn than a regular scarf. That is because they are 2 to 3 times wider and a little longer.

| Yarn Weight | 16x70" scarf | 20x80" scarf | 24x90" scarf |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sport (2) | 650 to 800 yds | 850 to 1,050 yds | 1,100 to 1,400 yds |
| DK (3) | 550 to 680 yds | 720 to 900 yds | 950 to 1,200 yds |
| Worsted (4) | 450 to 560 yds | 600 to 750 yds | 780 to 1,000 yds |
| Bulky (5) | 350 to 440 yds | 470 to 590 yds | 610 to 780 yds |
| Super Bulky (6) | 250 to 320 yds | 340 to 430 yds | 440 to 570 yds |

The [Yarn Calculator](/yarn-calculator) gives you an exact number based on your gauge and size. Enter your swatch measurements and target size. The tool tells you the total yardage and how many skeins you need.

**Pro tip:** Worsted weight is the best choice for a blanket scarf. It gives you a good mix of warmth, weight, and speed. Bulky weight works up faster, but the scarf ends up heavier and harder to wear. DK weight makes a lighter, drapier scarf, but it takes more time.

## What Stitch Patterns Work Best?

Blanket scarves look best in simple stitch patterns that have some texture. The pattern should also look good on both sides. Since the scarf wraps around your neck, both sides will show.

**Best reversible stitch patterns:**

**Garter stitch (knit):** Knit every single row. It is reversible, lies flat, and has great texture. This is the simplest choice and one of the most popular for blanket scarves.

**Seed stitch (knit):** Alternate knit and purl stitches, then switch the order every row. This makes a bumpy, textured surface that looks the same on both sides.

**Half double crochet (crochet):** Simple, reversible, and slightly textured. This is the go-to crochet stitch for blanket scarves.

**Linen stitch or moss stitch:** Alternating knit and slip stitches create a fabric that looks woven. It is reversible and looks great in variegated yarn.

**Herringbone stitch (crochet):** This creates a diagonal texture. It looks fancy but uses a simple repeat.

**Avoid:** Stockinette, because it curls at the edges unless you add a border. Avoid cables on one side only, since they are not reversible. Avoid very open lace, because blanket scarves need some density to stay warm.

## What Are the Best Tips and Common Mistakes?

**Add a border for stockinette.** If you want to use stockinette for the main body, add 3 to 5 stitches of garter stitch or seed stitch along each edge. This stops the edges from curling. Without a border, a stockinette blanket scarf rolls up into a tube.

**Choose a yarn that washes well.** Blanket scarves get dirty. Think coffee spills, food drips, and everyday wear. Use machine-washable yarn like superwash wool, acrylic, or a cotton blend. A hand-wash-only blanket scarf is one that never gets washed.

**Weigh your yarn as you go.** Running out of yarn near the end of a big project is very frustrating. Weigh your remaining yarn when you reach the halfway point. If you have used more than half, you will need to shorten the scarf or add more yarn.

**Block the finished scarf.** Even a garter stitch scarf looks better after blocking. Soak it for 15 minutes, press it in a towel, pin it to your target size, and let it dry flat. The stitches even out and the edges straighten.

**Add fringe or tassels (optional).** Fringe adds 20 to 40 yards per end, so 40 to 80 yards total. Cut 6 to 8 inch strands, fold each one in half, pull it through an edge stitch, and tighten it. Tassels use 15 to 25 yards each.

**Common mistakes:**
- Underestimating yarn. A blanket scarf uses 600 to 1,000 yards in worsted weight, not 300.
- Choosing a stitch pattern that is not reversible.
- Skipping the swatch. Gauge errors get bigger over 80 inches of length.
- Making it too wide for the yarn weight. A 24-inch bulky scarf can weigh 2 or more pounds.
- Forgetting to block. Without blocking, the edges look wavy and the stitches look uneven.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How wide should a blanket scarf be?

Most blanket scarves are 16 to 24 inches wide. The most popular width is 20 inches. That gives you enough fabric to drape as a shawl or fold as a cowl without looking too bulky. Petite frames look best at 16 to 18 inches. Larger frames or those who want more coverage do well at 22 to 24 inches. Anything wider than 24 inches starts to feel more like a lap blanket.

### Can I use any stitch pattern for a blanket scarf?

Use reversible stitch patterns, since both sides show when the scarf is wrapped. Garter stitch, seed stitch, half double crochet, and linen stitch all work well. Avoid stockinette without a border because it curls. Avoid heavy cables or textures on one side only. If you want a non-reversible stitch, add 3 to 5 stitches of garter or seed stitch along each edge.

### How long does it take to make a blanket scarf?

In worsted weight, plan for 20 to 30 hours for a 20 by 80 inch scarf. In bulky, plan for 10 to 15 hours. In super bulky, plan for 6 to 10 hours. Blanket scarves use simple stitches with no shaping, increases, or decreases. All the time goes into the actual stitching. If you stitch for 2 hours a day, a worsted blanket scarf takes about 10 to 15 days.

### What's the difference between a blanket scarf and a wrap?

The main difference is size and purpose. A blanket scarf is rectangular and 16 to 24 inches wide. You wear it around your neck or drape it over your shoulders like a shawl. A wrap is wider, usually 30 or more inches, and you wear it mainly over your shoulders. A wrap is closer to a small blanket. A blanket scarf is closer to a very wide scarf. Both are made the same way: a large rectangle.
