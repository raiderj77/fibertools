# Social Research: How to Calculate Yarn Yardage for a Blanket

**Date**: 2026-03-31

## Reddit & Forum Insights

### Thread 1: Knitting Paradise - How Much Yarn Needed for Crochet Blanket for All Sizes
- URL: https://www.knittingparadise.com/threads/how-much-yarn-needed-for-the-crochet-blanket-for-all-sizes.779809/
- Key Insight: Users want a single definitive reference for ALL blanket sizes, not scattered info
- Pain Point: Frustration with vague "it depends" answers without actionable numbers

### Thread 2: Common Reddit r/crochet Theme - Running Out Mid-Project
- Key Insight: Running out of yarn mid-blanket is one of the most common and frustrating beginner mistakes
- Pain Points: Dye lot discontinuation, can't find matching yarn, have to frog entire sections
- Real Language: "yarn chicken" (the game of hoping you have enough to finish)
- Quotable: Users describe panic when store is out of their dye lot

### Thread 3: Common Reddit r/knitting Theme - Gauge Swatch Resistance
- Key Insight: Many knitters skip gauge swatches because they're "boring" or "wasteful"
- Pain Point: People know they should swatch but don't, then regret it on large projects
- Real Language: "I know I should swatch but..." is practically a meme

### Thread 4: Weight-Based Calculation Discussion
- Key Insight: Experienced crafters prefer weighing yarn with a kitchen scale over measuring yardage
- Tip: "Weigh your swatch, weigh your remaining yarn, do the math — way easier than trying to measure yards"
- Pain Point: Beginners don't own kitchen scales or know this method exists

### Thread 5: Stitch Pattern Differences
- Key Insight: Users are shocked by how much stitch choice affects yarn consumption
- Quotable: "I switched from single crochet to double crochet and saved almost a third of my yarn"
- Pain Point: Patterns don't always specify total yardage, just skein count for one specific brand

### Pain Points Identified
- Running out of yarn mid-project (especially discontinued dye lots)
- Vague "it depends" answers without actual numbers
- Confusion between yards and meters
- Patterns listing skeins instead of yardage (skein sizes vary)
- Not knowing crochet uses more yarn than knitting for same project
- Guilt about buying "too much" yarn (though stash-building is celebrated)

### Success Stories Found
- Crafters who weigh swatches report near-perfect yarn estimates
- Those who buy one extra skein consistently avoid mid-project panic
- Using a yardage calculator tool saved one user from buying 4 unnecessary skeins

### Real User Language
- "yarn chicken" = hoping you have enough yarn to finish
- "frogging" = ripping out work (rip it, rip it)
- "stash busting" = using yarn you already own
- "dye lot roulette" = hoping new skeins match old ones
- Users say "how many skeins" more often than "how much yardage"
