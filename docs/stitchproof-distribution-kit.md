# StitchProof Distribution Kit

## Positioning

StitchProof is a free, browser-only arithmetic checker for common US amigurumi round notation. It compares stitches consumed, stitches created, and the total written in the pattern. It deliberately refuses unsupported notation instead of guessing.

Do not describe it as a complete pattern validator. It does not prove gauge, shaping, assembly, clarity, fit, or safety.

## Longer community post

I am testing a free tool called StitchProof for checking amigurumi round math. It supports common US notation such as sc, hdc, dc, increases, decreases, magic rings, and numbered repeats. It shows how many stitches each round consumes and creates and compares that result with the total written at the end of the round.

The pattern text stays in the browser and is not uploaded or saved. This first version is intentionally limited: it checks arithmetic, not whether the finished shape, gauge, assembly, or safety instructions are correct. Unsupported notation is marked as not verified.

If you try it, I would especially like to know which real notation it cannot read and whether a complete audit, private saved projects, or a downloadable report would be useful. Please do not share pattern text you do not own or have permission to share.

## Outreach guardrails

- Check each community's current self-promotion rules before posting.
- Post once in a relevant discussion; do not mass-post or repeat the same message.
- Disclose that the poster owns or operates FiberTools.
- Do not invent users, reviews, testimonials, expertise, or results.
- Do not claim the checker verifies an entire pattern.
- Do not ask people to paste copyrighted patterns they cannot legally share.
- Answer questions as a product owner, not as a professional crochet authority.
- Do not automate replies, direct messages, votes, or community engagement.
- Stop outreach if moderators object or the post is removed.

## Feedback record

For each real response, record only:

- date
- source
- requested capability
- whether willingness to pay was yes, maybe, no, or not stated
- whether the person appears to be a maker, tester, or designer
- a short de-identified problem summary

Do not copy names, email addresses, private pattern text, or message transcripts into AI prompts or the experiment ledger.
