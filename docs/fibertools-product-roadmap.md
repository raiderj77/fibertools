# FiberTools Product Roadmap

## Executive rule

FiberTools grows as one connected product. New modules must prove demand before they receive paid infrastructure. Free calculators remain useful without an account or email gate.

## Phase 1 — StitchProof demand test

Build now:

- free amigurumi round-math checker
- transparent consumed-versus-created stitch math
- strict unsupported-notation responses instead of AI guesses
- private, browser-only processing
- consented aggregate usage measurement with no pattern contents
- crawlable page, examples, limitations, FAQs, sources, and internal links

Expansion gate:

- at least 25 non-owner checker runs or 5 returning users in a complete 28-day window
- at least 5 explicit requests for complete audits, saved projects, or professional reports before paid infrastructure begins

## Phase 2 — StitchProof paid pilot

Build only after the Phase 1 gate:

- complete multi-section pattern audits
- revision comparison
- downloadable audit reports
- server-verified Stripe entitlements
- clear monthly usage caps
- failure-safe credit handling

Do not add persistent uploads at this stage. Process files ephemerally unless saved projects are separately validated.

## Phase 3 — Unified project workspace

Build only after repeat paid usage:

- accounts and secure saved projects
- encrypted private file storage
- revision history
- materials, inventory, cost, and progress records
- export and deletion controls

Before launch, complete a privacy threat model, retention policy, deletion test, backup/restore test, and access-control test suite.

## Phase 4 — CanvasKit

Start with manual color-region selection and deterministic calculations:

- canvas dimensions and mesh
- stitch type and strand count
- thread by color
- owned-versus-missing inventory
- projected cost, substitutions, and leftovers

Automatic image recognition remains out of scope until manual kitting is accurate and users complete paid projects with it.

## Phase 5 — Designer and Shop

Build only after individual Pro revenue is repeatable:

- team accounts
- batch audits
- branded reports and calculator embeds
- customer project and kitting links
- commercial-use terms and higher usage limits

## Deferred

- pattern generation
- garment grading
- advanced lace parsing
- automatic painted-canvas recognition
- marketplaces
- mobile applications
- social networking
- AI-written patterns
- public pattern hosting

## Pricing hypothesis, not a promise

The proposed $9 Pro, $19 Designer, and $49 Shop tiers remain research hypotheses. Publish prices only after usage, processing cost, support burden, entitlement security, and willingness-to-pay interviews support them.
